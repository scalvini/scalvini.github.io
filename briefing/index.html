The eurozone is caught in a classic stagflationary bind. Energy-driven inflation (Brent above $100) is running well above target, but the real economy is now contracting for the first time since late 2024. For Italian assets specifically, the risk is that ECB tightening to combat imported inflation pushes BTP yields higher at a time when the fiscal outlook is already stretched. The Meloni government has limited room to cushion the blow, particularly with defence spending commitments rising.
